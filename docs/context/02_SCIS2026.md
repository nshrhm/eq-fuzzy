# SCIS 2026 context

## Role

SCIS 2026 is the **persona x temperature factorial deconfounding** workstream inside the private `eq-fuzzy` monorepo.

The current SCIS directories are placeholders only. Do not add fake configs, prompts, analysis code, or manuscript claims before the factorial design is fixed.

## Main question

How much of the observed score variation is attributable to persona, temperature, and their interaction?

## Scope

Use:

- fully crossed persona x temperature design
- condition tables that separate persona wording from decoding temperature
- representative model subset suitable for a conference-scale experiment
- variance, valid-output rate, fuzzy entropy, effect sizes, and interaction summaries
- interaction plots, heatmaps, and main-effect tables

Do not use SCIS to claim:

- multilingual human-grounded alignment rankings
- benchmark superiority over existing emotion benchmarks
- annotation-workflow value for SPReAD1000

## Required separation from ICECCME

SCIS must not reuse the ICECCME neutral-reader setup as its main design. ICECCME can keep persona sensitivity as secondary analysis, but SCIS owns the independent crossing of persona and temperature.

SCIS should not use Japanese human reference alignment or EN/ZH drift as its primary endpoint.

## Required separation from ICICIC

SCIS may mention benchmark context only as background. It should not center matched comparison, coverage maps, or external benchmark positioning.

## Manuscript discipline

SCIS can say:

- earlier persona-assigned temperature designs confound two sources of variation
- independent crossing enables clearer reporting of main effects and interactions
- fuzzy entropy and variance can be interpreted more cleanly when design factors are separated

SCIS should not say:

- causal inference is fully solved
- human validity has been proven
- cross-lingual generalization has been established
